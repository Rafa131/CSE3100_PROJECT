Venera Program (Soviet Union):

Objective: To study the atmosphere, surface, and environment of Venus.
Mission Highlights: The Venera program consisted of multiple missions from 1961 to 1983. Venera 7 (1970) became the first spacecraft to successfully land on another planet and transmit data from its surface. Subsequent missions, such as Venera 9 and Venera 10, provided the first images of Venus' surface.

Magellan (NASA):

Objective: To map the surface of Venus in detail using radar imaging.
Mission Highlights:The Magellan spacecraft was launched in 1989 and orbited Venus from 1990 to 1994. It used synthetic aperture radar to create high-resolution maps of Venus' surface, revealing details of its volcanoes, impact craters, and tectonic features.

Venus Express (ESA):

Objective: To study the atmosphere, climate, and surface of Venus
Mission Highlights: Launched in 2005, Venus Express orbited Venus from 2006 to 2014. It provided valuable data on Venus' atmosphere, including its composition, circulation patterns, and the presence of volcanic activity. The mission also studied the planet's plasma environment and conducted investigations into the greenhouse effect on Venus.

Akatsuki (JAXA):

Objective: To study the atmosphere and climate dynamics of Venus.
Mission Highlights: Akatsuki was launched in 2010 and entered orbit around Venus in 2015 after a failed attempt in 2010. It has been studying Venus' cloud layers, atmospheric circulation, and weather patterns. The mission aims to understand the mechanisms behind Venus' super-rotating atmosphere and investigate the presence of lightning.

These robotic missions have significantly contributed to our understanding of Venus and its environment. While there haven't been crewed missions to Venus yet, future plans and concepts for crewed exploration of Venus may be considered as technology and knowledge advances in the future. Please note that for the latest updates and information on Venus missions, it is best to consult official space agency websites and reputable sources.
